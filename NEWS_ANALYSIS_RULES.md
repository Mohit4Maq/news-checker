## Core Definitions

### Factual News
**Definition**: News that presents verifiable information, supported by evidence, with clear attribution, minimal bias, and adherence to journalistic standards.

**Key Characteristics**:
- Multiple independent sources confirm the information
- Clear distinction between facts and opinions
- Transparent about sources and methodology
- Corrections are made when errors are discovered
- No hidden agenda or manipulation

### Propaganda
**Definition**: Information, especially of a biased or misleading nature, used to promote or publicize a particular political cause, point of view, or agenda, often at the expense of truth and objectivity.

**Key Characteristics**:
- Emotional manipulation over factual presentation
- Selective presentation of facts
- Appeals to fear, anger, or prejudice
- Lack of credible sources or misrepresentation of sources
- Clear political, commercial, or ideological agenda
- Resistance to correction or fact-checking

### Misinformation
**Definition**: False or inaccurate information shared without malicious intent.

### Disinformation
**Definition**: Deliberately false information spread with intent to deceive or mislead.

## Beneficiary & Hidden Agenda Detection

### Key Questions to Ask:
1. **Who is mentioned in the news?**
   - List all people, organizations, companies, political parties mentioned
   - What are their roles and interests?

2. **Who benefits from this news?**
   - Direct beneficiaries (people/entities directly mentioned)
   - Indirect beneficiaries (people/entities not mentioned but who gain)
   - Political beneficiaries (who gains politically?)
   - Economic beneficiaries (who gains financially?)
   - Reputational beneficiaries (who's image is improved?)

3. **What connections exist?**
   - Media ownership connections
   - Business relationships
   - Political affiliations
   - Financial interests
   - Family/personal relationships

4. **What's being hidden?**
   - What real news is being obscured?
   - What bigger story is this distracting from?
   - What accountability issues are being avoided?
   - What questions are NOT being asked?

5. **Why this timing?**
   - Why is this news being reported now?
   - What else is happening that this might distract from?
   - What elections, policy decisions, or events are upcoming?

### Red Flags:
- News that only benefits one person/party/company
- News that appears to be timed strategically
- News that avoids asking tough questions about beneficiaries
- News that doesn't disclose potential conflicts of interest
- News that seems to distract from more important issues

## Propaganda Indicators

### 1. Emotional Manipulation
- **Excessive use of loaded language**: Words designed to trigger emotional responses (e.g., "shocking", "outrageous", "betrayal", "conspiracy")
- **Fear-mongering**: Exaggerated threats or dangers without evidence
- **Appeal to emotions over logic**: Prioritizes emotional impact over factual accuracy
- **Sensational headlines**: Clickbait-style headlines that don't match article content

### 2. Source Issues
- **Anonymous or unverifiable sources**: Claims without attribution
- **Single source dependency**: Information only from one source with no verification
- **Misattributed quotes**: Quotes taken out of context or falsely attributed
- **Circular references**: Multiple sources citing each other without original verification

### 3. Logical Fallacies
- **Ad hominem attacks**: Attacking the person rather than the argument
- **False dichotomies**: Presenting only two extreme options
- **Strawman arguments**: Misrepresenting opposing views
- **Correlation vs causation**: Implying causation where only correlation exists
- **Cherry-picking**: Selecting only data that supports a particular view

### 4. Bias Indicators
- **One-sided presentation**: Only one perspective presented
- **Omission of key facts**: Important context deliberately left out
- **Loaded questions**: Questions that assume unproven facts
- **False balance**: Giving equal weight to unequal evidence

### 5. Agenda-Driven Content
- **Political alignment**: Clear promotion of specific political party/ideology
- **Commercial interests**: Promotion of products/services disguised as news
- **Religious/communal angle**: Content designed to divide communities
- **Foreign influence**: Content promoting foreign interests over national interests

### 6. Factual Distortions
- **Outdated information**: Presenting old news as current
- **Geographic misrepresentation**: Wrong location or context
- **Number manipulation**: Misleading statistics or data visualization
- **Photo/video manipulation**: Misleading images or deepfakes

## Factual News Criteria

### 1. Source Verification
- **Multiple independent sources**: Information confirmed by at least 2-3 independent sources
- **Primary sources**: Direct access to original documents, statements, or events
- **Reputable news organizations**: Established media with fact-checking processes
- **Expert verification**: Information verified by subject matter experts

### 2. Transparency
- **Clear attribution**: All claims properly attributed to sources
- **Methodology disclosure**: How information was gathered and verified
- **Correction policy**: Clear process for correcting errors
- **Editorial independence**: Clear separation between news and opinion

### 3. Balance and Context
- **Multiple perspectives**: Different viewpoints presented fairly
- **Historical context**: Relevant background information provided
- **Nuanced analysis**: Acknowledges complexity rather than oversimplification
- **Uncertainty acknowledgment**: Admits when information is incomplete

### 4. Journalistic Standards
- **Fact-checking**: Information verified before publication
- **Editorial oversight**: Multiple layers of review
- **Ethical guidelines**: Adherence to journalistic ethics
- **No conflicts of interest**: Transparent about potential biases

## India-Specific Relevance Framework

### 1. Direct Impact Categories

#### A. Policy & Governance
- **Government policies**: New laws, regulations, or policy changes
- **Administrative decisions**: Bureaucratic actions affecting citizens
- **Judicial decisions**: Court rulings with wide implications
- **Election-related**: Information about elections, candidates, voting

#### B. Economic Impact
- **GDP and growth**: Economic indicators affecting livelihoods
- **Employment**: Job creation, unemployment, labor market changes
- **Inflation**: Price changes affecting daily life
- **Taxation**: Changes in tax policies
- **Infrastructure**: Development projects affecting communities

#### C. Social Issues
- **Education**: Changes in education policy, access, quality
- **Healthcare**: Public health, medical facilities, health policies
- **Social welfare**: Schemes, benefits, social security
- **Caste/Religion**: Issues affecting social harmony
- **Gender issues**: Women's rights, safety, equality

#### D. Security & Safety
- **National security**: Threats, defense, border issues
- **Internal security**: Law and order, terrorism, extremism
- **Cybersecurity**: Digital threats, data privacy
- **Disaster management**: Natural disasters, emergency response

### 2. Indirect Impact Categories

#### A. Regional Relevance
- **State-specific**: News affecting specific states/regions
- **Local governance**: Municipal, panchayat-level issues
- **Regional development**: Infrastructure, industry in specific regions

#### B. Sectoral Impact
- **Agriculture**: Farming, rural economy, farmer issues
- **Industry**: Manufacturing, services, business environment
- **Technology**: Digital India, tech policy, innovation
- **Environment**: Climate change, pollution, conservation

#### C. International Relations
- **Foreign policy**: India's relations with other countries
- **Trade**: International trade agreements, exports/imports
- **Diaspora**: Issues affecting Indian communities abroad
- **Global events**: International events affecting India

### 3. Relevance Scoring

**High Relevance (8-10)**:
- Directly affects majority of Indian citizens
- Impacts national policy or governance
- Affects fundamental rights or constitutional values
- Has immediate economic or social impact

**Medium Relevance (5-7)**:
- Affects significant portion of population
- Regional or sectoral impact
- Indirect policy implications
- Medium-term consequences

**Low Relevance (1-4)**:
- Affects small, specific groups
- Minimal policy impact
- Long-term or speculative impact
- Primarily of interest value

**No Relevance (0)**:
- No connection to India or Indians
- Purely foreign news with no Indian angle
- Entertainment/gossip with no policy impact

## Categorization System

### Primary Categories

#### 1. FACTUAL NEWS
**Sub-categories**:
- **Verified Factual**: Multiple sources confirm, high credibility
- **Likely Factual**: Strong evidence, minor uncertainties
- **Contextual Factual**: Factual but needs more context

**Criteria**:
- Score ≥ 7/10 on factual verification
- Multiple independent sources
- Transparent methodology
- Minimal bias

#### 2. PROPAGANDA
**Sub-categories**:
- **Political Propaganda**: Promotes specific political agenda
- **Communal Propaganda**: Divides communities on religious/caste lines
- **Commercial Propaganda**: Promotes products/services disguised as news
- **Foreign Propaganda**: Promotes foreign interests over Indian interests
- **Misinformation Campaign**: Deliberate spread of false information

**Criteria**:
- Score ≥ 6/10 on propaganda indicators
- Clear agenda-driven content
- Emotional manipulation
- Factual distortions

#### 3. MISINFORMATION
**Sub-categories**:
- **Unverified Claims**: Information not yet verified
- **Outdated Information**: Old news presented as current
- **Misleading Context**: True facts presented misleadingly
- **False Information**: Incorrect facts shared unintentionally

**Criteria**:
- Score 4-6/10 on factual verification
- No clear malicious intent
- May be corrected with fact-checking

#### 4. OPINION/ANALYSIS
**Sub-categories**:
- **Balanced Analysis**: Multiple perspectives, well-reasoned
- **Biased Opinion**: One-sided but clearly labeled as opinion
- **Editorial**: Editorial position of publication

**Criteria**:
- Clearly labeled as opinion/analysis
- Transparent about perspective
- Based on facts even if interpretation differs

#### 5. SATIRE/PARODY
**Sub-categories**:
- **Satirical News**: Humorous take on current events
- **Parody**: Imitation for comedic effect

**Criteria**:
- Clearly labeled as satire
- Not intended to deceive

## Analysis Methodology

### Step 1: Source Analysis
1. **Identify primary source**: Who originally reported this?
2. **Check source credibility**: Reputation, history, fact-checking record
3. **Verify source claims**: Can sources be independently verified?
4. **Check for conflicts of interest**: Financial, political, or other biases

### Step 2: Content Analysis
1. **Fact extraction**: Identify all factual claims
2. **Emotional language detection**: Identify loaded words, fear-mongering
3. **Logical fallacy detection**: Identify reasoning errors
4. **Bias detection**: Identify one-sidedness, omissions

### Step 3: Cross-Verification
1. **Multiple source check**: Do other sources report the same?
2. **Expert verification**: What do subject matter experts say?
3. **Fact-checking databases**: Check against known fact-checking organizations
4. **Historical context**: Compare with past similar events

### Step 4: India-Specific Analysis
1. **Relevance assessment**: How does this affect India/Indians?
2. **Policy impact**: Does this relate to Indian policies/laws?
3. **Cultural context**: Cultural sensitivity and accuracy
4. **Regional relevance**: Which regions/states are affected?

### Step 5: Propaganda Detection
1. **Agenda identification**: What agenda is being promoted?
2. **Manipulation techniques**: What techniques are used?
3. **Target audience**: Who is this designed to influence?
4. **Impact assessment**: What would be the impact if believed?

## Fact-Checking Guidelines

### 1. Claim Verification Process
- **Primary source check**: Go to original source
- **Expert consultation**: Consult subject matter experts
- **Data verification**: Verify statistics, numbers, dates
- **Image/video verification**: Reverse image search, metadata check
- **Quote verification**: Verify quotes in original context

### 2. Red Flags for False Claims
- Too good/too bad to be true
- Confirms existing biases perfectly
- No credible sources
- Spreads rapidly on social media
- Appeals primarily to emotions
- Contradicts established facts without evidence

### 3. Verification Tools
- **Fact-checking organizations**: Alt News, Factly, Boom, AFP Fact Check
- **Government databases**: Official government websites, RTI responses
- **Academic sources**: Peer-reviewed research, academic institutions
- **International fact-checkers**: For global claims

### 4. India-Specific Fact-Checking
- **Government data**: Official statistics, reports
- **RTI responses**: Right to Information Act data
- **Court documents**: Judgments, affidavits
- **Parliamentary records**: Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha proceedings
- **State government sources**: State-specific official data

## Bias Detection

### 1. Political Bias
- **Party affiliation**: Clear support for specific party
- **Ideological slant**: Left, right, center positioning
- **Election timing**: Content timed around elections
- **Opposition bashing**: Excessive criticism of opposition

### 2. Beneficiary Analysis (CRITICAL)
- **People involved**: Who are the key people/entities mentioned in the news?
- **Direct beneficiaries**: Who directly benefits from this news being reported this way?
- **Indirect beneficiaries**: Who indirectly gains from this narrative?
- **Hidden connections**: Are there undisclosed relationships between:
  - News subjects and media owners
  - News subjects and advertisers/sponsors
  - News subjects and political parties
  - News subjects and business interests
- **Conflict of interest**: Do the people involved have financial, political, or personal stakes?
- **Real news hidden**: What important news might be hidden behind this story?
- **Agenda masking**: Is this story masking a bigger, more important story?
- **Timing analysis**: Why is this news being reported now? Who benefits from the timing?

### 2. Commercial Bias
- **Product promotion**: Disguised advertisements
- **Corporate interests**: Promoting specific companies
- **Sponsored content**: Undisclosed sponsorships
- **Conflict of interest**: Financial relationships not disclosed

### 3. Religious/Communal Bias
- **Religious targeting**: Negative portrayal of specific religions
- **Communal angle**: Content designed to divide communities
- **Stereotyping**: Generalizations about communities
- **Historical distortion**: Misrepresentation of historical events

### 4. Regional Bias
- **State favoritism**: Unfair promotion of specific states
- **Language bias**: Content favoring specific languages/regions
- **Urban vs rural**: Bias toward urban or rural perspectives

### 5. Foreign Influence
- **Foreign funding**: Content funded by foreign entities
- **Foreign agenda**: Promoting foreign interests
- **Proxy narratives**: Foreign narratives repackaged for India

## Source Credibility Assessment

### High Credibility Sources (Score: 8-10)
- Established mainstream media with fact-checking
- Government official sources (with verification)
- Peer-reviewed academic sources
- Reputable fact-checking organizations
- Primary documents (court orders, official reports)

### Medium Credibility Sources (Score: 5-7)
- Regional media with good track record
- Expert opinions (with verification of expertise)
- NGO reports (with methodology transparency)
- International media (with India-specific verification)

### Low Credibility Sources (Score: 1-4)
- Unverified social media posts
- Anonymous sources
- Known propaganda outlets
- Sources with history of false claims
- Unverified blogs/websites

### Red Flag Sources (Score: 0)
- Known fake news factories
- Sources with malicious intent
- Deepfakes or manipulated media
- Completely unverifiable claims

## Scoring System

### Overall News Quality Score (0-100)

#### Factual Accuracy (0-30 points)
- **30 points**: Multiple independent verification, primary sources
- **20 points**: Single credible source, likely accurate
- **10 points**: Unverified but plausible
- **0 points**: False or unverifiable

#### Source Credibility (0-20 points)
- **20 points**: Highly credible, established sources
- **15 points**: Credible sources with minor concerns
- **10 points**: Mixed credibility
- **5 points**: Low credibility sources
- **0 points**: Unreliable or malicious sources

#### Bias Level (0-15 points)
- **15 points**: Minimal bias, balanced presentation
- **10 points**: Some bias but acknowledged
- **5 points**: Clear bias, one-sided
- **0 points**: Extreme bias, propaganda

#### Propaganda Indicators (0-15 points)
- **15 points**: No propaganda indicators
- **10 points**: Minor emotional language
- **5 points**: Some manipulation techniques
- **0 points**: Clear propaganda

#### India Relevance (0-20 points)
- **20 points**: Direct, high impact on India/Indians
- **15 points**: Significant relevance
- **10 points**: Moderate relevance
- **5 points**: Low relevance
- **0 points**: No relevance

### Final Categorization Based on Score

- **90-100**: Verified Factual News, High Relevance
- **75-89**: Likely Factual News, Good Relevance
- **60-74**: Mixed Quality, Moderate Relevance
- **45-59**: Questionable, Needs Verification
- **30-44**: Likely Misinformation
- **15-29**: Probable Propaganda
- **0-14**: Clear Propaganda/Misinformation

### India-Specific Relevance Score (0-10)
- **8-10**: High relevance - affects majority of Indians
- **5-7**: Medium relevance - affects significant portion
- **1-4**: Low relevance - affects specific groups
- **0**: No relevance to India

## Appendix: Key Terms Glossary

- **Fact-checking**: Process of verifying factual claims
- **Primary source**: Original source of information
- **Secondary source**: Source that reports on primary source
- **Bias**: Prejudice in favor of or against something
- **Propaganda**: Biased information used to promote agenda
- **Misinformation**: False information shared unintentionally
- **Disinformation**: Deliberately false information
- **Deepfake**: AI-generated fake media
- **Clickbait**: Sensational content designed to attract clicks
- **Echo chamber**: Environment where only similar views are heard
- **Filter bubble**: Algorithmic isolation of information
